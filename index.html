SERVICES

Plate Count Method

The plate count method is a standard microbiological technique used to estimate the number of viable bacteria or fungal cells in a sample.

  • Samples are diluted appropriately.
  • Aliquots are plated on nutrient agar plates.
  • Plates are incubated for a specific duration.
  • SmartColony app is used to count colonies and calculate CFU/mL.

Water Quality Analysis

Water quality analysis helps determine the physical, chemical, and biological characteristics of water samples to ensure safety and compliance with regulations.

  • Parameters like pH, turbidity, and dissolved oxygen are measured.
  • Samples are tested for contaminants such as bacteria or chemicals.
  • SmartColony app is integrated to record the results and generate reports.

Who Can Avail the Services:

Internal Clients: PUP faculty members, researchers, and students (both undergraduate and postgraduate).

External Clients: Researchers, faculty members, and students from other universities with existing MOUs with PUP RIST.

About Us

The Center for Natural Sciences and Resources Research (CNSRR) offers comprehensive analytical services for various industries and research projects. Our mission is to provide high-quality, reliable scientific analysis for environmental, biological, and chemical samples.

Our team of experts is committed to maintaining the highest standards of accuracy and precision in our results, helping our clients meet their goals and regulatory requirements.
